## Domain Context and Terminology

### Overall Purpose of the Problem Statement:
The overall purpose of the City Resource and Infrastructure Management system is to plan, monitor, execute, and control city infrastructure projects efficiently while ensuring proper utilization of public funds, accountability across departments, and minimal inconvenience to citizens.

### Primary Goals / Outcomes:
- Effective monitoring of city resources such as electricity and water
- Structured approval and execution of infrastructure projects
- Transparent and controlled budget allocation
- Accountability for project execution and quality
- Improved coordination between administration, departments, and field staff

## Core workflows

### Workflow 1: Infrastructure Project Approval
- **Trigger / Start Condition:** Public demand or identification of infrastructure requirement
- **Steps Involved:**
  - Requirement is raised by citizens or public representatives
  - Request reaches the City Administrator
  - City Administrator evaluates feasibility and impact
  - Project is forwarded to the Department Officer for planning
- **Outcome / End Condition:** Project approved for execution planning

### Workflow 2: Budget Allocation and Verification
- **Trigger / Start Condition:** Budget requirement raised for an approved project
- **Steps Involved:**
  - Field Engineers submit raw data and estimated budget
  - City Administrator forwards the budget request
  - Department Officer verifies equipment and alternatives
  - Multiple vendor quotations are collected
  - Budget is reviewed by CFO and higher authorities
  - Approved funds are released in stages
- **Outcome / End Condition:** Budget sanctioned or revised for execution

### Workflow 3: Project Execution and Validation
- **Trigger / Start Condition:** Work order issued after approval and funding
- **Steps Involved:**
  - Department Officer assigns tasks to Field Engineers
  - Field Engineers execute on-site work
  - Completion is reported to City Administrator
  - City Administrator validates the work
  - QC department performs quality verification
- **Outcome / End Condition:** Project completed with certification

## Rules, Constraints, and Exceptions

### Mandatory Rules or Policies:

- All purchases must be fully justified with technical and financial details
- GST number is mandatory for bill approval
- Multi-stage approval process is mandatory for budget allocations
- QC department certification is mandatory for project closure
- Preference is given to government vendors

### Constraints or Limitations:

- Fixed annual budget for each department
- Multiple approval levels cause delays
- Funds are released on a quarterly basis

### Common Exceptions or Edge Cases:

- Emergency repairs requiring fast-track approval
- Budget cuts imposed by finance authorities

### Situations Where Things Usually Go Wrong:

- Budget collapse leading to project delays
- Public interference during field work
